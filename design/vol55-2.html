Catch Magazine: VOL. 55 NO. 2

Catch is an undergraduate literary magazine published by Knox College in Galesburg, Illinois. Established in 1922 and officially named Catch in 1969, it is the oldest continuously published undergraduate literary journal in the United States. The magazine is funded by Knox College and run entirely by a student-led collective, publishing undergraduate fiction, poetry, creative nonfiction, drama, music, and visual art.

Behind the Design

For Volume 55, Number 2, the editorial staff selected a theme inspired by a specific method of printing: risograph.

The visuals draws from its characteristics such as rough texture, visible layering and imperfect registration. The goal was not to simulate risograph printing exactly, but to capture its material energy and sense of process within the constraints of student production.

Custom stickers

In addition to the magazine layout, a series of custom stickers was designed as part of the issue visual identity. Given full creative freedom, the sticker designs were kept minimal and conceptually tied to the division of content within the magazine. Each sticker functions as a modular extension of the publication, reinforcing the theme while remaining accessible and playful.

Together, the issue reflects the collaborative nature of Catch and the realities of student-led publishing, embracing experimentation, limitation, and collective authorship as core elements of the design.
