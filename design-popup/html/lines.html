Lines in the Shed is a poetry book designed for writer and scholar Ryan Tracy, whose work navigates social fracture, environmental anxiety, intimacy, and queer identity within contemporary American life.

Design Concept

The book's neon color palette responds directly to recurring imagery within the poems, including references to radiation, artificial light, contamination, and environmental anxietyâ€”the uneasy glow of crisis embedded in the everyday.

Typography & Layout

The title treatment is inspired by label-maker tape, a utilitarian material commonly used to mark tools and objects in a shed. This reference aligns with the book's core tension: the shed as a traditionally masculine space, reimagined as a private site for drag, self-expression, and transformation.
