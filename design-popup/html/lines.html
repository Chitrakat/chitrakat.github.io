Lines in the Shed is a poetry book designed for writer and scholar Ryan Tracy, whose work explores social fracture, environmental anxiety, intimacy, and queer identity within contemporary American life.

Design Concept

The design is built around the image of a shed in a state of decay. Rather than treating the shed as a stable or orderly structure, it is presented as something broken, overgrown, and eroded. This damaged environment reflects the emotional and social landscapes of the poems.

Color & Material

The color of the book responds directly to recurring imagery in the text, including references to radiation, artificial light, and environmental contamination, as well as organic elements such as moss.

Typography & Identity

Typography plays a central conceptual role. The title treatment is inspired by label maker tape, a utilitarian material used to mark tools and objects in a shed. The type grounds the book in a traditionally masculine space while reframing that space as a private site for drag, self-expression, and transformation. In this context, a system meant for categorization becomes a marker of identity.
