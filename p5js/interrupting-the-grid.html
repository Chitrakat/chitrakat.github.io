No Meaning Required

This piece started with the grid, the most basic unit in design, the thing you're taught to respect before you're taught to break. I set up the plotter at Knox myself, the first person to run it, and spent a lot of that time alone with the machine figuring out what it could do.

I wanted the work to mean something. I kept thinking about pulling in live data, wiring it to an API, giving the grid a reason to exist beyond itself. However my design professor, told me it didn't need to and that it was allowed to just look good. That permission changed the project.

What I built instead was simple: circles placed across a strict grid, their position and size driven by randomness, but never enough to break the structure holding them. Getting it to paper took hundreds of generated patterns before landing on the ones worth plotting.
